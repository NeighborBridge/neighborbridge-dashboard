4. Market Gaps — What Unmet Need Each Customer Group Has

Foster/kinship caregivers

No structured pre-evaluation readiness tool exists. Current options: piecemeal provider advice, online forums, word-of-mouth. No single resource explains what an FASD evaluation involves, what records to gather, why multiple visits may be needed, or how to prepare for the PCP conversation.

Biological parents

Most existing FASD materials assume confirmed prenatal alcohol exposure, creating defensiveness or shame. No "here's what we know and don't know" framing exists. No language-safe guide for parents who may feel blamed.

Adoptive/guardianship families

"Unknown exposure" pathway is underserved. Most materials require exposure confirmation. No guide addresses: no prenatal history available, but NDD concerns are present.

Pediatric PCPs

PCPs lack a simple framework for initiating the FASD conversation and preparing referrals. AAP toolkit exists but is clinician-facing, not family-facing. No clinic-ready conversation guide for the PCP to hand off or discuss with families.

CHWs / navigators

No standard FASD navigator conversation guide exists. CHWs working with complex-NDD families have no structured tool. Most navigate FASD questions ad-hoc.

System-level gap

No integrated readiness pathway exists. Families piece together info from CDC, state orgs, provider visits, and online forums. The tools exist in fragmented, non-integrated forms across different organizations and formats.

Preparing for a Possible FASD Evaluation

What Families Need to Know

Internal draft Family education Diagnostic readiness Non-PHI

Purpose

This internal draft explains what families may need to understand when preparing for a possible FASD evaluation. It is intended as family education and diagnostic-readiness support. It does not diagnose FASD, replace clinical evaluation, guarantee access to testing, or promise completion of a comprehensive diagnostic report.

Why FASD Evaluation May Take More Than One Visit

FASD evaluation may require information from multiple sources. A clinical visit may identify concerns and guide next steps, but may not complete the full diagnostic process. Needed information may come from schools, caregivers, prior records, or outside specialists. The timeline may depend on access to outside testing and records.

Information That May Be Helpful

  • 1. Developmental history
  • 2. School records / IEP / psychological or educational testing
  • 3. Behavioral health records when relevant
  • 4. Prenatal alcohol exposure documentation when available
  • 5. Neurocognitive / neurobehavioral testing
  • 6. Growth and physical exam information
  • 7. Facial-feature assessment when indicated
  • 8. Prior medical, developmental, or therapy records

Why Neurocognitive / Neurobehavioral Testing Matters

Even when prenatal alcohol exposure information or facial-feature assessment is available, adequate neurocognitive / neurobehavioral data may still be needed for a fuller FASD evaluation. These data often come from outside specialists, school-based evaluations, or neuropsychological testing.

What Families Can Start Gathering

  • - Copies of school evaluations, IEPs, or 504 plans
  • - Prior psychological or developmental testing
  • - Therapy records if available
  • - Medical and developmental history
  • - Caregiver observations about learning, behavior, attention, adaptive skills, and daily functioning
  • - Any available information about prenatal alcohol exposure, if known

Important Boundary

This handout is educational. It does not provide a diagnosis, guarantee access to testing, guarantee a comprehensive FASD diagnosis, or promise a court/DSS-ready report. Any clinical evaluation should occur through the appropriate clinical intake process.

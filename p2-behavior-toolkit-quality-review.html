P2 Behavior Toolkit Booklet — Quality Review

Stage 1 · Internal Only · 2026-05-22 · Nora Product R&D

1. Logical Flow

Verdict: ✅ Solid arc, one structural question.

The 10-page sequence moves well:

  • Pages 1-3: Orientation → reframing (behavior = communication) → foundation (brain-based expectations). Good opening triad.
  • Pages 4-5: Sensory → emotional regulation. Natural adjacency; sensory overload often precedes dysregulation.
  • Page 6: Trauma/grief/attachment. Placed correctly — after regulation but before practical school/home strategies.
  • Pages 7-8: School vs. home → de-escalation/structure. Practical application layer.
  • Pages 9-10: When to seek help → support planning. Closure and forward-looking.
✅ Strong: The arc moves from reframing → understanding → regulation → practical tools → professional guidance → planning. No major gaps in the conceptual sequence.
⚡ Consider: Pages 4 (sensory overload) and 5 (emotional regulation/co-regulation) may feel thematically similar to a caregiver. Could merge into one "Sensory & Emotional Regulation" page and use the freed slot for something else (see #3 below). Not critical at Stage 1.

2. Duplication / Overlap

Verdict: ✅ Minimal. Two flagged areas.

  • Page 4 vs. 5: Both discuss "meltdown" and "co-regulation." Page 4 frames it sensorily; Page 5 frames it emotionally. Distinct enough for Stage 1, but will need careful differentiation in design.
  • Page 8 (de-escalation) vs. Page 5 (co-regulation): "Lower your voice" (P8) overlaps with "model calm breathing" (P5). In design, P5 should stay emotional/internal, P8 should stay environmental/structural.
  • No other significant overlap across the 10 pages.

3. Missing Caregiver-Facing Concepts

Verdict: ⚠️ Two notable absences for Stage 1 consideration.

  • Executive function in daily life — Page 3 touches brain-based expectations but doesn't name executive function explicitly (working memory, task initiation, time blindness, organization). These are concrete caregiver pain points that could anchor one page or be woven into Pages 3, 7, and 8.
  • Caregiver self-care / burnout — Page 9 mentions caregiver well-being obliquely ("consider therapy for the caregiver too"), but there's no dedicated frame for caregiver fatigue, isolation, grief, or burnout. This is a common unmet need for FASD/NDD caregivers and could strengthen the booklet's credibility.
💡 Not urgent at Stage 1, but: If the booklet is intended to feel complete to caregivers, one of these (executive function or caregiver self-care) could replace a merged Page 4/5, or be added as a short front/back note.

4. Strongest Pages as Future Product Content

Verdict: Pages 2, 3, 5, 8 have the highest standalone value.

  • Strongest Page 2 — Behavior as Communication: The core reframe. Works as a standalone handout, brochure module, or caregiver intake sheet. Low risk, high clarity.
  • Strongest Page 3 — Brain-Based Expectations: Empowering but grounded. Helps caregivers shift from frustration to understanding. Key differentiator from generic parenting advice.
  • Strongest Page 5 — Emotional Regulation / Co-Regulation: Practical, teachable skill. Translates well into a 1-pager or workshop module.
  • Strongest Page 8 — De-escalation and Daily Structure: Most actionable. Caregivers can use this immediately. Highest "fix-it" risk — boundaries are well-placed but need reinforcement in design.

5. Higher Clinical/Legal/Safety Risk Pages

Verdict: Pages 6, 9 carry the most risk.

🔴 Page 6 — Trauma, Grief, Attachment, and Trust
  • Risk category: Clinical boundary, emotional loading, trigger potential.
  • Specific concerns: "Seek trauma-informed therapy support" is a clinical recommendation; needs careful framing as suggestion, not directive. Trauma/attachment language may trigger caregivers with unresolved grief. DSS/court boundary is correct but must hold in every revision.
  • Mitigation: Keep as caregiver education, not clinical guidance. Use "Some families find it helpful to..." rather than "Seek..." Clara review required.
🟡 Page 9 — When to Seek Professional Help
  • Risk category: Safety, liability, clinical threshold.
  • Specific concerns: Crisis line (988/911) is mandatory — correct. "Safety concerns, self-harm talk, aggression that endangers family" are well-framed. Risk is listing specific clinical indicators that could be interpreted as diagnostic thresholds.
  • Mitigation: Current framing is reasonable for Stage 1. Keep "consider" and "reach out" language. Ensure 988/911 is visually prominent in any designed version.

Medium risk Page 4 — Sensory Overload: Using PACT metaphor ("How is your engine running?") could be seen as clinical framing. Attribute properly if included in designed version.

6. Source Notes — Sufficiency for Stage 1

Verdict: ✅ Sufficient for Stage 1 concept work.

  • Each page has 2-3 authoritative source citations (CDC, AAP, CanFASD, FASD United).
  • All sources are public, non-paywalled, and current.
  • No source contains PHI, copied text, or copied images.
✅ Ready for Stage 1. What Stage 2 would need: Direct attribution per module, cross-reference to specific source pages/sections, and — if any page goes external — full citation formatting consistent with academic/clinical standards.

📋 Product Candidate — FASD Diagnostic Readiness Family Education Module

Status: Internal demo-stage · Validated direction (Holly/DSS conversation 2026-05-14) · Not productized from the event itself

Product: FASD Diagnostic Readiness Family Education Module

Plain-language title: "Preparing for a Possible FASD Evaluation: What Families Need to Know"

Description: A family-facing education module that explains what may be needed for a possible FASD evaluation, why neurocognitive / neurobehavioral testing matters, what records may be helpful, why outside specialty testing may be required, and why the diagnostic pathway may take time.

Primary users: caregivers/families preparing for possible FASD evaluation; CHWs or care navigators helping families understand next steps; pediatric clinical teams explaining the diagnostic-readiness process

User problem: Families may believe FASD diagnosis can be completed through one appointment or by one clinician, when in reality the process may require multiple evidence streams and outside testing. Families need understandable education on what information to gather and why the process may take time.

MVP scope: (1) One-page family education handout or internal demo page. (2) Explains FASD evaluation components. (3) Explains role of neurocognitive/neurobehavioral testing. (4) Lists common helpful records. (5) Explains that outside testing may be needed. (6) Explains that the module is educational, not a diagnosis or referral guarantee.

Not in scope: court-specific guidance · legal advice · forensic evaluation · DSS timeline management · diagnosis guarantee · public referral tool · official Contentnea policy · patient-specific care navigation · PHI collection

📋 Internal Docs Reader QA — Pre-Commit Rules

Root cause (2026-05-16): A document entry in const documents = {...} was closed with }; instead of },, and the top-level object was not properly closed. This caused the entire documents object to fail silently — sidebar (static HTML) still rendered, but documents[id] was undefined, so the reader panel stayed blank.

Required pre-commit checks (every internal-docs.html edit):

  1. Validate JS syntax — no unmatched braces, no missing commas between entries, no premature }; inside the documents object.
  2. Validate document object closure — top-level const documents = {...}; must be properly closed. Each entry ends with comma, except the final entry before the outer close.
  3. Validate ID matching — every sidebar data-doc value must match a key in the documents object. No orphans.
  4. Validate required fields — each document must have title, category, content (non-empty).
  5. Click-test 5 representative documents — Global MH Arc, NC Primary Site, Vinod Decision Memo, St. Louis Family, one caregiving doc.
  6. Add fallback behavior — if a document ID is missing or content is empty, the reader must show a visible error/placeholder (not fail silently).
  7. Use safer editing pattern — one document entry per commit when adding high-risk internal docs. Validate after every addition.

Required report format (every internal-docs.html change):

  • JS syntax validated: yes/no
  • Sidebar IDs matched to document keys: yes/no
  • Reader click-test completed: yes/no
  • Number of document keys detected
  • Commit hash

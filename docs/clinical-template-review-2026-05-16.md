## Important Cautions (hard rules, per Richard)

### Harm language (Critical)
- Do not overstate that high-intensity ABA is harmful in FASD unless supported by clear FASD-specific evidence.
- Permitted language if evidence supports it: "may require adaptation," "may be poorly tolerated," "may have diminishing clinical utility."
- **Do not use broad harm claims** (e.g., "potential iatrogenic harm," "may be harmful").

### CPT / Billing (Critical)
- **Do not hard-code CPT codes into the template.**
- Any CPT / prolonged-service / billing language must be reviewed by billing/admin and payer policy *before* use.
- Note: Older prolonged service codes (e.g., 99354/99355) have been **deleted** — payer-specific rules may vary.
- Template should focus on clinical content only; billing guidance is a separate deliverable.

### External Use (Critical)
- **Do not convert this into public-facing product material.**
- **Do not create payer-facing pitch language without Clara review** (AGENTS.md mandate applies).
